“Proclaim the Lord's death until he comes”

Now when the hour came, Jesus took his place at the table and the apostles joined him. And he said to them, “I have earnestly desired to eat this Passover with you before I suffer. For I tell you, I will not eat it again until it is fulfilled in the kingdom of God.” Then he took a cup, and after giving thanks he said, “Take this and divide it among yourselves. For I tell you that from now on I will not drink of the fruit of the vine until the kingdom of God comes.” Then he took bread, and after giving thanks he broke it and gave it to them, saying, “This is my body which is given for you. Do this in remembrance of me.” And in the same way he took the cup after they had eaten, saying, “This cup that is poured out for you is the new covenant in my blood."

--Luke 22:14-20 (NET)

After he had given thanks he broke it and said, “This is my body, which is for you. Do this in remembrance of me.” .... So then, my brothers and sisters, when you come together to eat, wait for one another.

--1 Corinthians 11:24, 33 (NET)

During the Last Supper meal, Jesus invited all of his disciples to share the same broken loaf and the same cup.

They ate this bread and drank the cup throughout the course of the meal. In particular:

  • At the beginning of the meal, Jesus invited them to divide the cup of wine among themselves.

  • At the end of the meal, Jesus offered them all a refill, as it were, as he began the time of teaching recorded in John chapter 15 and following.

Since wine isn't the sort of drink that one guzzles down, and the context of the entire thing is a meal (more precisely, a Passover sedar) the natural conclusion is that the disciples divided the wine at the beginning of the meal, sipped it throughout, and then were offered refills at the end of the meal to sip during the time that Jesus taught them.

Similarly, breaking the bread was done as a part of a meal that Jesus and the disciples took together.

In our second text, we see that this meal was an event that Jesus invited the whole Church in all ages to celebrate. In Corinth, they had gotten some significant details of how to implement this meal wrong, and so Paul was correcting them. But it is clear from the context in Corinthians that Paul understood that Jesus was inviting not just the first disciples to eat the meal, but all those in all ages who desire to walk as disciples.

I was on vacation recently and noticed these things, and for the first time in my walk as a Christian asked the question, “If this is how they had church, we don't we do that today?” In seeking to answer this question, the answers I found disturbed me, and more and more I became convinced that God's church (ekklesia) needs to assemble together according to the pattern revealed in Scripture.

So this document is my personal record of what the Lord is helping me to understand as I study the Word and attempt to discern God's pattern for his Assembly of believers. As such, it is written as a series of Bible studies, using an expository style in the form of a traditional preaching style as used by Edwards, Finney, and Moody combined with Protestant verse-by-verse exegesis.

But there is one thing this document is not, nor will it ever be. It is not comprehensive. I will attempt to write as comprehensively as I can. But I am just one person, and my own failings and shortcomings will also be reflected in this work.

Further, just as each disciple received their own piece of the loaf, and their own portion of the wine, I do not pretend to have all understanding of the Word nor the full anointing of the Holy Spirit. I have just the piece Jesus gave me. Consequently, as I write, while I will do my best to include thoughts and studies I have learned from others and while I welcome corrections and outside feedback, the result will by necessity reflect my own portion of the Bread and the Wine.

Nevertheless, I hope that this portion proves to be useful to others and provides a helpful basis for discussion and understanding. And ultimately I hope that by putting all of our portions together, we collectively may once again fully discern the complete Loaf and Cup which we have inherited from our Lord.
