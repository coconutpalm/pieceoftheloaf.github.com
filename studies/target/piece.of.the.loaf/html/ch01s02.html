1.2. The last Passover, a new family of faith

In the verses at the top, we see that Jesus told his disciples to prepare the Passover for all of them. And then he sat down to eat the Passover with them.

But God had spoken through Moses that the Passover was to be eaten "according to their families." According to the Law of Moses, Jesus should have dismissed his disciples to all go to their families and celebrate Passover with them, and then to gather back together again later.

But he didn't, because he had something very special to accomplish:

While they were eating, Jesus took bread, and after giving thanks he broke it, gave it to his disciples, and said, “Take, eat, this is my body.” And after taking the cup and giving thanks, he gave it to them, saying, “Drink from it, all of you, for this is my blood, the blood of the covenant...

--Matthew 26:26-28 (NET)

After eating the bread and drinking the cup, the bread and the fruit of the vine literally became a part of each disciple. In the words of Adam, then, the disciples spiritually were "bone of my bones and flesh of my flesh", the Body of Christ, taken out of his side.

Jesus had worked a miracle. The Law commanded that they meet together as a family to celebrate Passover. Jesus had just created a new family, the Family of God. It was some days before this family became known as the ekklesia, the assembly, the Church.

At the beginning, it was just a family—eating a meal together, listening to the head of the house instruct them in the way they should go.

Tell the whole community of Israel, ‘In the tenth day of this month they each must take a lamb for themselves according to their families – a lamb for each household.

--Exodus 12:3 (NET)

So the disciples did as Jesus had instructed them, and they prepared the Passover. When it was evening, he took his place at the table with the twelve.

--Matthew 26:19-20 (NET)
